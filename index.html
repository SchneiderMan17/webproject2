Edwin's Heirlooms- My Side Business

The Business

Edwin's Heirlooms was established in February of this year. It is my attempt to develop woodworking skills in the hope of one day owning this business full time. The art of woodworking is based on the legacy my grandpa has left in my life. That legacy has inspired me to create a business around the projects he made. If this business becomes successful, or not, ultimately I hope to inspire the generations after me to hold onto important skills in woodworking crafstmanship.

Exploring Edwin

Edwin is a retired Navy Officer with 20 years service. He was married to his high school sweetheart for more than 50 years. Once he retired from the Navy, He was all in on woodworking. Some of his earliest and best projects included a treehouse for some grandkids that lived nearby, and a gazebo on the property of a kennel that he owned and operated. Even at an early age I remember being in my grandfathers work shop. Being involved in Boy Scouts gave us lots of projects to work on. Things like pinewood derby cars and the final Eagle Scout project were hot on his to-dos. There are many opportunities for character growth when working with wood like attention to detail, patience, planning, and dealing with failure. These are lessons I hope to teach to my kids and grandkids, all through the environment of a workshop and wood.

The Art of Woodworking

As I grew up I was reminded time and time again the unlimited potential of wood and an idea as we were gifted rocking horses, wagons, and keepsake boxes. You wouldn't have to look very far to see other projects he had made to adorn his own house. All of the projects he made, to give or to keep were made with quality in mind. Sturdy wood, dovetail joints, with paints and varnishes perfectly administered meant that nothing short of disaster would see his work ruined. That's why the motto for this company: "Love Handed Down" adds the foundation that every piece this company makes will have the same precision and attention to detail as the next, as if I were giving each of these pieces to my own great-grand children, or putting it in my own house. Heirlooms stand the test of time and are often made with sturdy materials, both to hold the weight of the object itself and all the memories it holds with it.
